Accessing the Admin Panel

The admin panel is a private web page that only you can access. You manage everything — events, subscribers, settings — from here. No coding required.

Admin panel URL:

https://your-site.pages.dev/admin/
Tip
Bookmark this URL in your browser so you can get back to it quickly.
2

First-Time Setup — Creating Your Admin Account

The very first time anyone visits the admin panel, they will be taken to a Setup page to create the admin account. This only happens once.

  • 1

    Go to /admin/ in your browser. You will be automatically redirected to the setup page.

  • 2

    Enter a Username (minimum 3 characters, e.g. admin).

  • 3

    Enter a Password of at least 8 characters. Use the strength indicator to choose a strong one. A mix of letters, numbers and symbols is recommended.

  • 4

    Confirm your password — type it again to make sure there are no typos.

  • 5

    Click "Create Admin Account". You will be redirected to the login page automatically.

Important
Write down or save your username and password somewhere safe. If you forget them, a developer will need to reset them via the Cloudflare dashboard.
Security Note
The setup page is permanently disabled once an account is created. Nobody else can create an account after yours is set up.
3

Logging In

  • 1

    Go to /admin/. The login form will appear.

  • 2

    Enter your Username and Password.

  • 3

    Click "Sign In". You will be taken to the dashboard.

Session Duration
Your login session lasts 24 hours. After that you will need to log in again.
4

Dashboard Overview

The dashboard has four sections accessible from the left sidebar:

SectionWhat you can do
Events Add, edit, and delete seminar events. See stats for upcoming vs. past events.
Subscribers View everyone who signed up for email updates. Export as CSV. Remove subscribers.
Tools Seed initial data, copy RSS feed URL, copy subscriber emails, clear all events.
Account Change your username and password.
5

Managing Events

Adding a new event:

  • 1

    Click the blue "Add Event" button (top right of the Events page).

  • 2

    Fill in the event details in the form that appears (see field guide below).

  • 3

    Click "Save Event". The event appears on the public website immediately.

Editing an event:

  • 1

    Find the event in the list. Use the search or filter controls at the top if needed.

  • 2

    Click the pencil icon (✏️) on the right side of the row.

  • 3

    Update the fields and click "Save Event".

Deleting an event:

  • 1

    Click the red bin icon (🗑) next to the event.

  • 2

    Confirm the deletion in the popup. This cannot be undone.

Event fields reference:

Date required
The date of the seminar (use the date picker)
Start / End Time
Defaults to 1:00 PM – 2:00 PM AEST
Speaker(s) required
Full name(s) including title, e.g. Dr Jane Smith
Institution
The speaker's university or organisation
Talk Title
Leave blank to show "TBA" on the website
Abstract
Full talk summary. Hidden behind a "Show Abstract" button.
Venue
e.g. "Parramatta South Campus EB.3.36" or "Via Zoom"
Zoom Link
Full Zoom URL — automatically activates 10 min before the event
Abstract PDF
Path to a PDF, e.g. Abstracts/speaker.pdf
6

Newsletter Subscribers

People who sign up on the public website appear in the Subscribers tab. From here you can:

  • View all subscribers — name, email, and date they signed up.
  • Export to CSV — download the list as a spreadsheet for mail merge or email campaigns.
  • Remove a subscriber — click the red bin icon next to their row.

Sending a newsletter:

Go to Tools → Send Newsletter → Copy All Emails. This copies all subscriber email addresses to your clipboard. Paste them into your email client (Outlook, Gmail, etc.) as BCC recipients, write your update, and send.

RSS Feed
The site also has a live RSS feed at /api/rss that automatically lists upcoming events. Share this URL with subscribers who prefer RSS readers.
7

Changing Your Username or Password

  • 1

    Click "Account" in the left sidebar.

  • 2

    Click "Change Credentials".

  • 3

    Enter your new username and a new password (minimum 8 characters).

  • 4

    Click "Save Changes". You will be logged out and must sign in with the new credentials.

Note
After saving, you are automatically logged out for security. Log back in with your new credentials immediately to make sure they work.
8

Troubleshooting

ProblemSolution
Forgot password Contact your developer to reset credentials via the Cloudflare dashboard.
Events not appearing on the website Check that the event was saved — go to the Events tab and look for it in the list. Try refreshing the public site.
Zoom link not showing as active The Zoom join button activates automatically 10 minutes before the event start time. It is disabled at other times by design.
Logged out unexpectedly Sessions expire after 24 hours. Simply log in again.
"Error saving event" message Make sure all required fields (Date and Speaker) are filled in. Check your internet connection.
Setup page appears again This should not happen once setup is complete. Contact your developer if it does.
